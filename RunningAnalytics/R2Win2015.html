Thursday, 1/1/2015 Morning Normal Run [ Top ]
Workout Data:
Total distance 9.17 Miles in 1:06:05 [7:12 per mile]
Comments New Years Day Res Run w/Max

Awesome run to kick off 2015. Met Max at Res 6 at 9, very chilly at first but not an issue after the first 2ish miles. I was sorta planning for us to mimic the long run route from Everett's from 2 weeks ago, but we took a few different trails and stumbled upon a great new area. Telephone pole trail next to the gradual uphill, then I think the back way to Kilkenny, instead of continuing we took some random trails and stumbled upon an amazing lookout point way above the top of the Red Loop. So fun to get "lost" in such a familiar place. Treacherous but awesome route down the side sliding (sledding) on leaves then back on the Red Loop, gradual uphill, and OAB at Res 6. We stopped a fair bit to traverse and explore the new terrain, one of the best parts was figuring out yet again that "it all connects". I loved this run for the route, didn't feel great on the hills but it was fasted. Immediately after went on a family hike at Case Mountain in Manchester (very close to the MRR course).

Saturday, 1/10/2015 Afternoon Race [ Top ]
Workout Data:
Total distance 1 Mile
Comments Hispanic Games Mile

Race supposed to be at 3:30, but actually closer to 3:25. There were two heats of the invite section, and I was in the slower one with Altopp and Soggard from Ridgefield. Felt rushed once I got in the pit and the only strides were on the track immediately before. Spikes felt tight in the toe box and tore up to tops of me feet a little as usual, the left came untied in the last two laps but didn't have an effect on anything. School record (until next Saturday) is .39 faster.

Pretty small race, got off the line purposely at the back and worked into things. Honestly, it was all a blur and over very quickly. Eventually moved up to the back of the front pack and closed pretty well to finish 4th. Never felt like I settled into pace great and didn't feel like I was holding my arms well. Soggard won at the line a second ahead and Altopp passed me in the last 100. The fast heat was so fast, 5 guys at 4:15 or below with the winner at 4:10.

Obviously, coming this close to the record and not getting it is disappointing. I could have made up a half second+ in so many places. I can't be too disappointed though, this was a great opportunity and great first race. I split 4:25 on this track at Nationals, so I'm in a very good position right now. In future races, I know I'll be more competition-oriented, I wasn't really racing to beat the other guys in this race which is unfortunate.

Wednesday, 1/21/2015 Morning Threshold [ Top ]
Workout Data:
Total distance 10 Miles
Comments 3 x 3200m w/2 min

Woke up at 7 to get the workout out of the way. Cold out, but I managed fine. I wasn't sure how this would go, but pleased with how the times felt and the amount of work I got in. Workout was 3x3200 in 10:45 with two minutes rest. Splits: 10:40, 10:44, 10:43. Reps 1 and 3 were backward which was a nice change. Everything was evenly paced throughout, focused on feeling relaxed with good turnover, and I got in a good mental zone. Probably the 5th mile (1st of the last rep) felt the best, didn't look a my watch until the mile (5:21) and it felt very relaxed aerobically. The 2 minute rest was plenty, I made clothing changes on both so not much jogging. Not sure how this compares to a normal mile threshold, but doing 6 at this pace was nice even though I had an extra minute of recovery.

Monday, 1/26/2015 Afternoon Interval Workout [ Top ]
Workout Data:
Total distance 11.1 Miles
Comments 4x2, 6x4, 4x2

Early dismissal, cold out and windy on the backstretch, flurries during the workout but no real accumulation. Ran from Max's, 3.1 warmup (to Hall then 2 mi loop), light drills. The track was perfect, not slippery at all; shoveling paid off! I missed the split button on my watch a few times today, but I have a good idea of what the actual splits were. First 4x2 was basically long strides, felt alright. 6x4 slated to be :65s, probably averaged :63.5-:64; I wasn't exactly sure on the recovery, so I took 3 min and focused more on quality and composure in the reps (could have done 2:30 rest comfortably but probably with a little slower splits). 4x2 fast after with a relatively short recovery, more for turnover and form than top-end speed work. 3 mi cooldown on 2 mi loop then back to Max's to hang with a good crew for a little. As a whole, surprised by and pleased with how good this workout went. Great mentality and composure on everything, got some good mileage in today too before the snow. Good indicator and practice relaxing form at mile pace, though CCCs will probably be my last mile of the season (sub-4:20?).
